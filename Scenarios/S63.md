### **1. EASY: The "Blind Spot" (Metrics & Exporters)**

**Scenario:** You deploy the Sentinel fraud detection engine using Apache Flink.
**The Problem:** It processes 10,000 events per second perfectly for three weeks. Then, at 2:00 AM, the JVM runs out of memory and the Flink TaskManager container violently dies. You look at the logs, but the logs only show the final death rattle (`java.lang.OutOfMemoryError`). You have absolutely no idea if it was a slow, multi-day memory leak or a sudden, massive spike in traffic.
**Task:** Expose the internal hardware and software metrics of your distributed systems before they crash.

#### **Solution: Prometheus Exporters (The Pull Model)**

Prometheus is a time-series database designed strictly for system metrics. It does not wait for your servers to send data; it actively goes out and interrogates them.

**The Architecture:**

1. **The Exporter:** You attach a lightweight metrics agent (an Exporter) to your Kafka brokers, your Flink nodes, and your underlying EC2 instances.
2. **The Endpoint:** These exporters expose a simple `/metrics` HTTP endpoint. If you curl this endpoint, it returns a plain-text list of raw vitals: `cpu_usage_seconds 45.2`, `jvm_memory_bytes_used 2048000`.
3. **The Scrape:** Every 15 seconds, the central Prometheus server reaches out to all 500 of your servers simultaneously, scrapes that `/metrics` page, and writes the numbers into its highly compressed time-series database.

### **2. MEDIUM: The "Black Box" (Time-Series Visualization)**

**Scenario:** You are building a Streamlit application that simulates the internal workings of Apache Spark, specifically visualizing how components like the driver and executors manage data.
**The Problem:** To make the simulator realistic and useful for debugging, you need to show how JVM garbage collection and heap memory behave under heavy shuffling load. Looking at a raw wall of text logs (`INFO: GC paused 0.05s`) is completely unreadable for a human interface.
**Task:** Translate millions of raw metric data points into human-readable visual trends.

#### **Solution: Grafana and PromQL**

Prometheus stores the data, but **Grafana** makes it readable. They are the ultimate open-source observability duo.

**The Architecture:**
Grafana connects directly to the Prometheus database. You use a specialized language called **PromQL** (Prometheus Query Language) to shape the data on the fly.

**The Workflow (The Memory Dashboard):**
Instead of just plotting the raw memory bytes, you write a PromQL query to calculate the *rate of change* in your simulated Spark executors.

* **The Query:** `rate(jvm_memory_bytes_used{job="spark-executor"}[5m])`
* **The Result:** Grafana renders a beautiful, real-time line chart. If your Spark simulator causes a memory leak, the graph will show a relentless, 45-degree upward slope over the last three hours. You can visually identify the exact minute the leak started and correlate it to a specific code deployment.

### **3. HARD: The "Silent Drop" (Anomaly Detection & AlertManager)**

**Scenario:** Your Kafka cluster is healthy. Your Flink consumers are alive. CPU and memory are totally stable.
**The Problem:** An upstream software engineer accidentally deployed a bug to the checkout microservice, and it stopped emitting `transaction` events.
**The Disaster:** No servers crashed. In fact, your Flink CPU usage looks *great* because it has no work to do. Traditional monitoring ("Is the server up?") stays green. But the business is losing millions of rows of critical data, and you won't know until the daily report is completely empty tomorrow morning.
**Task:** Architect an alert based on mathematical deviations in data flow, not just binary up/down statuses.

#### **Solution: PromQL Anomaly Detection and Alertmanager**

We configure Prometheus to evaluate the *behavior* of the data stream, not just the hardware.

**The Architecture:**

1. **The Behavioral Metric:** We track the exact number of messages Kafka is receiving per second: `kafka_server_messages_in_total`.
2. **The Mathematical Rule:** We define a Prometheus Alerting Rule that compares the current 5-minute ingestion rate to the historical average from exactly one week ago.
```text
rate(kafka_server_messages_in_total[5m]) 
< 
(rate(kafka_server_messages_in_total[5m] offset 1w) * 0.5)

```


3. **The Alertmanager:** If this mathematical condition is true (current volume is less than 50% of last week's volume at this exact time of day), Prometheus fires an event to **Alertmanager**.
4. **The Routing:** Alertmanager silences duplicates, formats the message, and instantly triggers a PagerDuty alarm, waking you up to fix the data flow before the business is even awake.
